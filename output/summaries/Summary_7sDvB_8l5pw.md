## Overview
This episode of Mata Najwa covers President Prabowo's direct field visit to flood-affected areas, likely in North Sumatra's Tamiang region, where he personally assessed post-disaster recovery efforts. The video documents the President's on-ground inspection of infrastructure damage, his conversations with affected farmers and displaced residents, and his immediate policy responses to address housing, agricultural, and economic needs. The footage reveals the scale of destruction - with entire rice fields destroyed just one week before harvest and hundreds of homes lost along with their land foundations.

## Key Highlights

President Prabowo demonstrated hands-on leadership by personally visiting disaster sites and directly engaging with affected communities. During his field inspection, he observed infrastructure repair efforts including power line restoration requiring 60 local residents to manually pull 10-ton cables due to the inability to use heavy machinery in the damaged terrain. The President witnessed firsthand the challenges of aid distribution, where relief supplies were reaching main command posts but struggling to reach remote villages, necessitating helicopter operations for effective delivery to isolated communities.

The agricultural devastation emerged as a critical concern during the President's interactions with local farmers. Rice fields across the region were completely destroyed just one week before harvest, representing a total loss of what would have been the community's primary income source for seven months. Farmers reported needing rice assistance for the next seven months as they rebuild from zero, highlighting the long-term economic impact beyond immediate disaster relief. The President responded with concrete assurances, promising that damaged farmland would be restored, food supplies would be sent from other regions, and importantly, that farmers' existing agricultural loans would be forgiven due to the force majeure circumstances.

Housing displacement presented another major challenge, with 100 homes completely lost along with the land beneath them due to erosion and flooding. Affected residents expressed immediate concerns about temporary shelter arrangements and long-term relocation prospects, as they were currently staying with relatives in nearby areas but recognized this was unsustainable. The President addressed this directly by instructing that state-owned land be identified for relocation purposes and explicitly forbidding regional governments from purchasing private land for disaster housing, while warning sternly against anyone attempting to profit from the disaster.

## Implications
This direct presidential engagement demonstrates a significant shift toward hands-on crisis management in Indonesian disaster response. The immediate policy decisions on loan forgiveness and state land allocation suggest the government is prepared to absorb substantial costs for comprehensive recovery, while the President's strong warning against disaster profiteering indicates awareness of potential corruption risks in relief efforts.
