## Overview
Aceh Governor Zainal Abidin delivered an emotional late-night interview with Najwa Shihab about the devastating floods that struck Aceh province, which he compared to a "second tsunami." The disaster, caused by eight days and nights of continuous rain, has affected 18 districts and cities across Aceh, with Tamiang district being the worst hit. The Governor described unusual aspects of this flood, including strange discoloration and odors in the water, dead animals that normally survive floods, and the complete destruction of wooden houses. Ten days into the emergency response, infrastructure is gradually being restored, but many areas remain isolated and in desperate need of aid.

## Key Highlights

The Governor painted a harrowing picture of the flood's impact, describing it as unlike any previous disaster. The floodwaters turned black and caused itching and burning sensations, while emitting strange odors. Most disturbing was the discovery that animals typically adapted to floods - large snakes, monitor lizards, and fish - were found dead, which the Governor found inexplicable. Bodies couldn't be buried for 2-3 days due to flooding, requiring them to be tied to trees and bridges until higher ground became accessible. Unlike the 2004 tsunami which lasted only 20 minutes, this disaster persisted for over a week, with waist-deep mud covering entire areas and wooden houses completely swept away without trace.

Recovery efforts have made progress in the ten days since the emergency was declared, with electricity restored to many areas and smaller bridges reopened for public access. However, four districts remain largely inaccessible by land, requiring sea and air transport. The Governor has requested additional helicopters from President Jokowi to reach isolated communities, particularly in East Aceh where less than 1% of affected areas have been reached. International aid has arrived from Malaysia and China, including medical teams and supplies, arranged directly through provincial government relationships rather than central government channels.

The Governor issued sharp criticism of several district heads for their inadequate response to the crisis, suggesting some had abandoned their responsibilities during the emergency. He specifically mentioned leaders who were "not focused" and failed to take responsibility, with one allegedly engaging in karaoke activities during the disaster. Looking ahead, rehabilitation and reconstruction will require massive efforts, with the most urgent current needs being medicines, cooking equipment, religious supplies, blankets, clothing, and small LPG gas canisters for affected families.

## Implications
This disaster highlights both the vulnerability of Aceh's infrastructure to extreme weather and the critical importance of local leadership during emergencies. The Governor's direct criticism of district officials and his decision to seek international aid independently of Jakarta suggests tensions between different levels of government in disaster response, potentially influencing future emergency management protocols in Indonesia.
