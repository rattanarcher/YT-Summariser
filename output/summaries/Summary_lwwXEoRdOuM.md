## Overview

Tempo's investigative program "Bocor Alus Politik" examined the acid attack on human rights activist Andri Yunus, with civil society coalition representative Dimas providing analysis on the incident. The discussion revealed that this attack was part of a systematic pattern of terror against human rights defenders in Indonesia, with investigators uncovering evidence of an organized network involving 11 perpetrators rather than the initially reported smaller number.

## Key Highlights

The program framed the acid attack on Andri Yunus within the broader historical context of systematic terror against human rights activists in Indonesia. Dimas emphasized that such incidents are not isolated events but represent a continuing pattern throughout the republic's history, making thorough investigation essential to prevent future occurrences. The attack was specifically linked to Yunus's identity and activities as a human rights defender, suggesting targeted persecution of civil society activists.

Civil society coalition investigators have uncovered substantial evidence pointing to a coordinated operation behind the attack. Their findings align with police investigations but reveal a more extensive network than initially understood. The investigation discovered that the attack involved systematic planning and organization, with evidence pointing to 11 perpetrators rather than the "more than 4" initially reported by authorities. This suggests a well-resourced and carefully planned operation against the human rights activist.

The systematic nature of the attack raises serious concerns about the safety of civil society activists and human rights defenders in Indonesia. The organized approach and multiple perpetrators indicate this was not a random act of violence but a calculated attempt to intimidate those working in human rights advocacy.

## Implications

This case highlights the ongoing threats faced by human rights defenders in Indonesia and suggests the existence of organized networks targeting civil society activists. The revelation of systematic coordination behind the attack may prompt broader investigations into threats against human rights workers and could influence government policies on activist protection.
