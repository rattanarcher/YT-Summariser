## Overview

This Bocor Alus Politik episode reveals significant political maneuvering around Indonesia's NasDem party, with reports of potential acquisition attempts by Gerindra party leader and President Prabowo Subianto. The discussion centers on a February 2024 meeting between Prabowo and NasDem chairman Surya Paloh at Hambalang, where they allegedly discussed raising the parliamentary threshold from 4% to 8% and the future of both parties. The video also explores how NasDem's business interests have been affected since supporting Anies Baswedan in the 2024 presidential election, and details an exodus of senior NasDem politicians to the Indonesian Solidarity Party (PSI).

## Key Highlights

The most significant revelation concerns a secret February 2024 meeting between President Prabowo and NasDem chairman Surya Paloh at Hambalang, where they reportedly discussed raising Indonesia's parliamentary threshold to 8%. This increase would pose serious challenges for medium-sized parties like NasDem, which only secured 9.66% of votes in 2024, raising questions about whether such a move would actually harm NasDem's future electoral prospects. The hosts suggest this meeting was part of broader discussions about potential cooperation or merger between Gerindra and NasDem, though no concrete agreements were reached. Sources from both parties provided conflicting accounts of who initiated these discussions, with some claiming Gerindra made offers that were ultimately rejected by Surya Paloh.

The episode details significant business pressures facing Surya Paloh's media and business empire following NasDem's support for Anies Baswedan in the presidential election. His catering company Indokarter, which serves Freeport mining operations and represents over 70% of his business interests, reportedly faced disruptions. Additionally, his Indonesia One tower project in Jakarta's Thamrin area experienced funding difficulties, with bank credit reportedly being cancelled just weeks before finalization in 2022-2023. However, sources indicate that after the Hambalang meeting with Prabowo, credit flows resumed and construction activity restarted, suggesting a potential quid pro quo arrangement.

A systematic brain drain is occurring within NasDem, with senior politicians defecting to PSI under what sources describe as a coordinated recruitment campaign. Key figures including former deputy chairman Ahmad Ali, Bestari Barus, and regional leaders like Hendra Joni have switched parties, often receiving immediate leadership positions in PSI. Internal NasDem communications reportedly joke about waiting for "love letters" from Ahmad Ali, indicating widespread awareness of the recruitment efforts. This exodus is attributed to internal dissatisfaction with Surya Paloh's apparent grooming of his son Prananda Paloh as successor, creating factional tensions within the party.

## Implications

These developments highlight the vulnerability of personality-based political parties in Indonesia and suggest potential realignment ahead of the 2029 elections. The combination of business pressure, internal succession disputes, and systematic talent poaching could significantly weaken NasDem's position, potentially forcing Surya Paloh into unfavorable political arrangements with the ruling coalition.
