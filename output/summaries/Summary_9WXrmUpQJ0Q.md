## Overview
In this extensive political interview, KH Yahya Staquf, Chairman of Nahdlatul Ulama (NU), Indonesia's largest Islamic organization, discussed his transformative agenda for NU and its relationship with Indonesian politics. Speaking with host Akbar Faizal, Yahya outlined his efforts to create clear boundaries between NU as an organization and political parties, while addressing internal challenges including attempts to remove him from leadership and the legal case involving his brother, Religious Affairs Minister Yaqut Cholil Qoumas.

## Key Highlights

Yahya revealed that NU's constituency has grown dramatically from 27% in 2005 to 56.9% in 2023 according to LSI surveys, representing more than double growth in just 18 years compared to minimal growth in the previous 50 years. He emphasized that this rapid expansion requires careful analysis, as NU has transformed from a traditional pesantren-based network into a much broader demographic reality. The organization now maintains approximately 7,000 sub-district level offices and 61,000 village-level offices across Indonesia, giving it unprecedented grassroots reach.

The NU chairman articulated three fundamental reform agendas he introduced upon taking office: organizational transformation from a "very casual and unregulated" structure to a more systematic one; repositioning NU to maintain distance from political competition and power struggles; and revitalizing NU's international role. He stressed that unlike the era of Abdurrahman Wahid (Gus Dur), when political institutions were dysfunctional and NU filled the gap, Indonesia now has a more liberal political system where parties should be empowered rather than bypassed by religious organizations.

Regarding internal NU politics, Yahya defended his decision to maintain distance from his brother's legal case, stating he chose to protect NU's institutional integrity over family loyalty. He acknowledged this stance has created internal tensions and attempts to remove him from leadership, but maintained it represents the necessary sacrifice for organizational transformation. He confirmed plans to seek re-election in an upcoming congress, viewing his reform agenda as incomplete and requiring additional time to fulfill his promises to NU members.

## Implications

Yahya's repositioning of NU represents a significant shift in Indonesian Islamic politics, potentially reducing the organization's direct political influence while strengthening its institutional independence. His approach could reshape how religious organizations engage with political power in Indonesia's democracy, setting precedent for clearer separation between religious and political authority that other organizations might follow.
