## Overview

This video from Tempo's "Bocor Alus Politik" program discusses the organized nature of the terror attack on activist Andrie Kelompok, revealing evidence of a coordinated operation involving multiple participants. The hosts analyze investigative findings that suggest the attack was carried out by an organized group rather than individual actors, with evidence pointing to systematic planning including road clearing and coordination signals. The discussion raises questions about whether civil society investigative findings align with official police investigations, particularly regarding four individuals detained by military police.

## Key Highlights

The investigation into the Andrie Kelompok terror attack has uncovered evidence of a sophisticated, coordinated operation involving significantly more people than initially apparent. According to the investigative findings discussed, the attack involved approximately a dozen or more participants who played various roles in the execution. The evidence suggests systematic planning, including the deliberate clearing of roads before the attack occurred. Investigators noted that while the road was busy with traffic beforehand, it became mysteriously empty during the critical moments of the incident, indicating that someone was actively managing the scene to facilitate the attack.

The hosts reveal that their count suggests the operation could not have involved fewer than 10 people, with their analysis pointing to approximately 13 individuals participating in the coordinated attack. This finding challenges any narrative that the incident was carried out by lone actors or a small group. The discussion indicates that the attack required careful coordination, including individuals responsible for giving signals and managing various aspects of the operation to ensure its success.

A significant question remains about the alignment between civil society investigations and official police findings. The program raises concerns about whether the four individuals detained by military police (Puspom) correspond to the same suspects identified through independent civil society investigations. This discrepancy highlights potential gaps between official and independent investigative efforts, suggesting the need for greater transparency and coordination in uncovering the full scope of this organized terror operation.

## Implications

This revelation of organized coordination in the Andrie Kelompok attack has serious implications for Indonesian security and civil society protection. The sophisticated nature of the operation suggests institutional or network-level involvement that could indicate broader threats to activists and civil society organizations. The apparent disconnect between civil society findings and official investigations raises questions about the comprehensiveness and transparency of state-led inquiries into attacks on activists.
