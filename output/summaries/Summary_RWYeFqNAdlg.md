## Overview

This video features Akbar Faizal providing commentary on the acid attack against Andrie Yunus, a prominent Indonesian activist. Faizal expresses full support for police investigation into the incident and suggests that military personnel may be involved based on evidence patterns and Yunus's recent activities focusing on military-related issues. He emphasizes that while he doesn't believe the military institution itself is responsible, individual members operating in the field may be implicated.

## Key Highlights

Faizal argues that the police investigation should proceed without hesitation regardless of who might be involved, specifically mentioning the possibility of military personnel being behind the attack. He bases this assertion on two key factors: evidence apparently pointing in that direction, and the fact that Yunus's most prominent activities over the past year have involved military-related matters. The host stresses that he is not accusing the military as an institution but rather suggesting individual members operating in the field may be responsible.

The commentary extends beyond the Yunus case to highlight what Faizal characterizes as a broader pattern of violence against activists and journalists. He mentions several other recent cases of attacks, including the murder of Pak Hermanto and assaults on various individuals including Firian Aurelo, Sherly, Iqbal Damanik, DJ Doni, Tempo staff, and Tio, the head of KMUGM. This framing suggests systemic intimidation tactics against those engaged in investigative or activist work.

Faizal criticizes what he sees as military personnel's deviation from their protective mandate, arguing that instead of safeguarding the nation's people, some members are participating in activities that harm civilians. He pledges full support for police efforts to uncover the truth behind these incidents.

## Implications

This commentary reflects growing concerns about intimidation tactics against activists and journalists in Indonesia, particularly those investigating military-related issues. The call for thorough investigation regardless of perpetrator identity suggests tensions between civilian oversight and military accountability, which remains a sensitive issue in Indonesian governance and civil society relations.
