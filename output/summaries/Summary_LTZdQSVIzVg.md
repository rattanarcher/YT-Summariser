## Overview
This Mata Najwa video documents the devastating impact of severe flooding in North Sumatra (Sumut), specifically in the Besitang area of Langkat Regency. The segment captures raw footage of flood victims struggling to survive among debris and ruins, with water levels reaching up to 3 meters in some areas. Local officials report approximately 1,300 families affected across the region, with residents forced to evacuate to mosques and other shelters. The video highlights the challenges faced by both victims and relief workers trying to deliver aid to isolated communities cut off by floodwaters.

## Key Highlights

The flooding struck without warning during nighttime hours, forcing residents to abandon their homes with little more than the clothes on their backs. One particularly poignant story features a widow named Mawadda Hasibuan, a teacher who lives alone with her child, whose home was completely destroyed. The footage shows her standing amid the rubble of what was once her house, expressing disbelief at the scale of destruction. Large rocks and debris were carried by the floodwaters from nearby rivers, something local residents say they had never witnessed before in previous flooding events.

Relief efforts faced significant obstacles due to infrastructure damage and the sheer scale of the disaster. Young volunteers from Besitang spent three days stuck in Stabat, the regency capital, trying to arrange transportation for aid supplies to reach affected communities. The main toll road was impassable due to high flood levels, forcing relief workers to seek alternative routes and rent specialized high-clearance vehicles. Communication networks were severed, making coordination of rescue and relief operations extremely difficult in the initial days following the disaster.

Local village head (Lurah) described having to risk his own life to coordinate relief efforts, using fishing boats to cross what had become like "an ocean" of floodwater to collect aid from the Langkat Regent waiting on higher ground. The priority was initially saving lives, with hundreds of evacuees taking shelter at the Grand Mosque of Sibah. The official emphasized the urgent need for continued assistance as the community begins the long process of recovery and rebuilding.

## Implications

This flooding represents one of the most severe natural disasters to hit North Sumatra in recent memory, exposing vulnerabilities in Indonesia's disaster preparedness and infrastructure resilience. The scale of destruction and the challenges in delivering aid highlight ongoing issues with disaster management coordination between local and regional authorities, which could influence future policy discussions on climate adaptation and emergency response protocols.
