## Overview
A Tempo investigation reveals that individuals suspected of involvement in the acid attack on Andri Yunus gathered at a coordination post located around the Malawer area in Jakarta. Government agencies and the Ministry of Defense confirmed that this location is officially registered as a state facility under the Strategic Intelligence Agency (BAIS), raising serious questions about the misuse of state resources in what appears to be a criminal conspiracy.

## Key Highlights

The investigation traced the movements and communications of suspects in the Andri Yunus acid attack case back to a coordination post in the Malawer area. When cross-referenced with government agencies and the Ministry of Defense, official documents confirmed that this location is registered under the Strategic Intelligence Agency (BAIS), indicating it is state property. This revelation suggests that state facilities may have been used to facilitate or coordinate criminal activities, representing a significant abuse of government resources.

The report emphasizes the importance of tracking the suspects' movements throughout Jakarta, noting that if they can be proven to have originated from this BAIS facility, it would establish this location as their operational base. Investigators are relying on CCTV evidence to trace the suspects' routes and determine whether they departed from this state facility, which could be classified as either a residence or office. However, the investigation has encountered obstacles regarding vehicle identification, as two separate sources confirmed that the license plates used by the suspects are unrecognized in official databases, suggesting they may be fraudulent or unregistered.

## Implications
This case raises serious concerns about potential involvement of state intelligence apparatus in criminal activities and the misuse of government facilities. The connection between a BAIS facility and suspects in an acid attack case could indicate broader issues with accountability and oversight within Indonesia's intelligence services.
