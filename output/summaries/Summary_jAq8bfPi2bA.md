# KARNI 'PRESIDEN ILC' ILYAS. KESAKSIAN ATAS 7 PRESIDEN
**Channel:** Akbar Faizal Uncensored
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jAq8bfPi2bA

## Overview
This interview features veteran Indonesian journalist Karni Ilyas discussing his experiences covering seven Indonesian presidents throughout his career. The conversation provides insights into press freedom, political relationships, and the evolution of Indonesian media from the authoritarian Suharto era through to President Prabowo's administration.

## Key Participants
- **Host**: Akbar Faizal (politician and host of "Akbar Faizal Uncensored")  
- **Guest**: Karni Ilyas (veteran journalist, former editor of Tempo magazine, creator of Indonesia Lawyers Club/ILC at TV One)

## Key Points & Timestamps

- [01:24] **Karni's Role in Creating Politicians**: Akbar credits Karni with helping launch the careers of post-1998 reform era politicians through his TV platforms, stating politicians weren't considered legitimate until appearing on Karni's shows

- [07:03] **Seven Presidents Covered**: Karni confirms he has journalistically covered all seven Indonesian presidents from Suharto to Prabowo

- [07:39] **Most Media-Savvy President**: Karni identifies B.J. Habibie as the president who best understood media, praising him for introducing press freedom after the repressive New Order era

- [10:47] **Suharto's Media Repression**: Details the extreme censorship under Suharto, including daily prohibition lists sent to media outlets and the banning of reporting on a student rafting accident simply because it was named after Sudomo

- [13:28] **Karni's Near-Arrest**: Reveals he was almost arrested (T.O. - "taken out") during Suharto's era but was saved by the 1998 reform movement

- [18:34] **Megawati's Treatment**: Describes positive relationship with President Megawati, including being invited on overseas trips in business class, though relations soured after editorial disputes

- [22:22] **SBY Relationship**: Recounts strong relationship with Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono, including exclusive coverage when SBY was dismissed as minister and during his presidential elections

- [28:35] **Jokowi's Distance**: Notes that Jokowi was never particularly close and became more distant due to critical coverage, especially regarding infrastructure promises

- [32:15] **Prabowo Interview Promise**: Reveals that President Prabowo promised him the first exclusive interview but it was cancelled by TV One directors on the scheduled day

- [34:18] **Offered Attorney General Position**: Confirms he was offered the position of Attorney General by President Megawati but declined after reflection

- [39:34] **ILC's Massive Success**: Describes Indonesia Lawyers Club as earning 2-3 billion rupiah per episode with venue costs alone reaching 300 million rupiah

- [48:40] **Critique of Modern Journalism**: Argues that contemporary journalists have lost integrity, mixing business and political interests into their reporting, causing public trust to erode

## Notable Quotes (translated)

1. [07:39] "Habibie, after we were so constrained by the repressiveness of the New Order... he created press freedom. And press freedom isn't just about being free - he never paid attention to what was written."

2. [34:50] "One morning I woke up and there was my assistant from that era... many people called to congratulate me. But the most problematic was Hendro Priyono's call... 'Hey, you're the Attorney General.'"

3. [48:13] "Journalists do not maintain integrity. So what they report has many interests in it - business interests, political interests, all kinds. Therefore they're not straight as a journalist should be."

## Analysis & Implications

This interview provides valuable historical perspective on Indonesian press freedom and political evolution since 1998. Karni's accounts reveal how media personalities wielded significant influence in shaping post-Suharto political discourse, while his critique of modern journalism highlights ongoing challenges with media credibility and public trust. His unfulfilled promise of interviewing President Prabowo suggests potential tensions between media independence and political access in contemporary Indonesia.
