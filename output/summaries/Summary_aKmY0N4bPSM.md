# Prabowo Debat Najwa Shihab soal Kritik Rakyat ke Pemerintah: Ini Kesempatan Saya Jawab!
**Channel:** KOMPASTV
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aKmY0N4bPSM

## Overview
This video captures a heated debate between Indonesian President Prabowo Subianto and prominent journalist Najwa Shihab regarding government criticism, civil liberties, and allegations of state intimidation against critics. The exchange reveals tensions over how the administration handles dissent and investigates incidents involving government critics.

## Key Participants
- **Prabowo Subianto**: President of Indonesia
- **Najwa Shihab**: Senior journalist and host, known for her critical interviews of political figures

## Key Points & Timestamps
- [00:00] **False Flag Operations**: Prabowo suggests some critical incidents may be provoked, comparing them to intelligence operations like "false flag" tactics used by Mossad against Palestinians
- [00:32] **Government Response Gaps**: Najwa argues that public speculation about authoritarian tendencies arises from lack of government answers and unresolved cases
- [01:04] **Presidential Guarantees**: Prabowo pledges to build a "civilized Indonesia" with clean police, prosecutors, and intelligence services, claiming he won't allow intimidation of critics
- [02:04] **Investigation Commitment**: The President promises thorough investigations "down to the actors" and gives direct guarantee that no citizens will be intimidated for criticizing government
- [02:35] **Independent Team Consideration**: When asked about forming independent investigation teams, Prabowo says he's open to considering it but criticizes NGOs that receive foreign funding
- [03:39] **Deep State Claims**: Prabowo reveals finding "deep state" elements, including director-generals who defied ministers and were subsequently dismissed
- [04:11] **BUMN Reform**: The President discusses ongoing cleanup of state-owned enterprises (BUMN), calling them "one of our biggest vulnerabilities"
- [05:51] **Regime Change Allegations**: Prabowo suggests some criticism is part of international destabilization efforts and "color revolution" tactics
- [06:22] **Distinguishing Criticism**: When asked how he differentiates between legitimate criticism and sedition, Prabowo points to August demonstrations where protesters attempted to burn government buildings
- [07:54] **Student Arrests**: Najwa challenges him on mass arrests of students and activists, noting research showing the largest such crackdown since the Reform era

## Notable Quotes (translated)
- [01:34] "Trust me, I was chosen by the people to defend the people."
- [02:04] "There must be no citizens who are intimidated or terrorized for criticizing the government. This is a direct guarantee from the President of the Republic of Indonesia."
- [08:24] "I swear to defend my people. I have risked my life many times. I cannot bear to leave this country in the hands of thieves and bandits when God calls me."

## Analysis & Implications
This exchange highlights the ongoing tension between press freedom advocates and the Prabowo administration over civil liberties and government accountability. The President's acknowledgment of "deep state" elements while simultaneously defending against authoritarianism allegations reflects the complex political dynamics in post-Suharto Indonesia. His willingness to engage in this confrontational interview format demonstrates both confidence in his position and recognition of the need to address growing criticism from civil society and media.
