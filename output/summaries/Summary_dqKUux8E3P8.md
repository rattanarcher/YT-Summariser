## Overview
This episode of Musyawarah hosted by Najwa Shihab and co-host Kak Jo covers several significant Indonesian issues while also featuring international perspectives. The discussion begins with Najwa's recent coverage of pro-Palestine demonstrations in New York during the UN General Assembly, moves to domestic concerns about the problematic implementation of Indonesia's Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) program, addresses the tragic collapse of a pesantren building in Sidoarjo that killed 67 students, and includes an interview with Professor Matthew Nicholson about Monash University Indonesia's expansion. The episode concludes with Kak Jo addressing his recent relationship status in response to social media speculation.

## Key Highlights

The episode opens with Najwa's firsthand account of massive pro-Palestine demonstrations in New York, where she chose to cover the 20,000-person protest rather than Netanyahu's UN speech. She describes a global movement of conscience, with demonstrations reaching 250,000 people in Amsterdam and over 100,000 in Sydney, representing what she calls the largest protest in modern Australian history. Najwa emphasizes that this isn't just about the past two years since October 7, 2023, but about 77 years of Palestinian suffering since the Nakba in 1948, when Israel declared independence and displaced hundreds of thousands of Palestinians who still cannot return to their ancestral lands.

The conversation shifts to Indonesia's troubled Free Nutritious Meals program, where numerous food poisoning cases have emerged due to uncertified kitchens and substandard food preparation. Najwa argues that while the program's intentions are good, the rushed implementation has created serious problems. She advocates for decentralizing the program to school kitchens rather than external providers and emphasizes the need for proper oversight. Drawing comparisons to successful international programs, she notes that Brazil took 40 years and South Korea 66 years to perfect their school meal systems, suggesting Indonesia needs patience and systematic implementation rather than rushing to meet targets.

The discussion becomes particularly somber when addressing the pesantren building collapse in Sidoarjo that killed 67 students during afternoon prayers. While the pesantren's caretaker attributed the tragedy to fate (takdir), both hosts argue that proper investigation is essential to determine structural failures and accountability. They emphasize that calling it fate cannot stop the search for truth about building violations, inadequate oversight, and systemic failures that may have contributed to this preventable tragedy.

## Implications

These discussions highlight critical governance challenges in Indonesia, from foreign policy positioning on international crises to domestic program implementation and regulatory oversight. The episode underscores how rushed policy implementation, inadequate supervision systems, and lack of proper accountability mechanisms continue to plague Indonesian public administration, potentially undermining well-intentioned programs and public safety.
