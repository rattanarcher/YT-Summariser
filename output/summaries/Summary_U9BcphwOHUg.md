## Overview

This video from Mata Najwa shows Najwa Shihab reporting from a flood evacuation center in Aceh, where she interviews displaced families who have lost their homes and belongings to severe flooding. The report captures personal stories of evacuation experiences, the complete destruction of homes, and the resilience of families, particularly children, who are adapting to life in the emergency shelter. The segment highlights both the immediate humanitarian crisis and the ongoing rescue efforts by evacuation teams working to reach stranded residents in remote flooded areas.

## Key Highlights

The video presents harrowing accounts from flood victims who were forced to abandon their homes with nothing but the clothes on their backs. One family describes how the water rose so quickly that they couldn't retrieve any belongings, leaving behind all their possessions including clothing, furniture, and cooking equipment. They now depend entirely on donations from others for basic necessities like food and clothing. The father explains that their house and everything inside has been completely destroyed, emphasizing that the most important thing was simply saving their lives rather than material possessions.

The evacuation process itself was chaotic and dangerous, with rescue teams describing scenes where stranded residents fought to board evacuation boats, with priority given to women and children. Some people were trapped for days in elevated positions like on top of tables, waiting for rescue teams to reach them. The most remote victims were found in rice fields, completely cut off and without food supplies, leading even the experienced boat operators to tears when witnessing their desperate conditions. The flooding was so severe that it mixed with mud and debris, making navigation extremely difficult and leaving entire houses displaced from their foundations.

Despite the trauma and loss, the resilience of the community, particularly the children, is striking. Young students who have been out of school for three to four days due to the disaster show remarkable adaptability, finding ways to play and maintain their spirits in the evacuation center. They engage in simple games and activities while expressing their desire to return to school, representing hope and normalcy amid the crisis.

## Implications

This report underscores the ongoing vulnerability of Indonesian communities to extreme weather events and the critical importance of disaster preparedness and response systems. The complete dependence of victims on community solidarity and donations highlights gaps in formal disaster relief infrastructure that could be addressed through policy improvements and better emergency planning.
