## Overview
This video features an investigation into the March 12, 2024 acid attack on Indonesian human rights activist Andrie Yunus, coordinator at Kontras. The discussion reveals evidence of military intelligence involvement in the attack, including a command chain reaching higher levels of the Indonesian Armed Forces (TNI). The panel discusses findings about a coordination post connected to military intelligence and critiques the government's response, particularly concerns about limiting accountability to only four alleged perpetrators while protecting higher-ranking officials.

## Key Highlights

The investigation reveals systematic surveillance and coordination preceding the acid attack on Andrie Yunus. Tempo's investigative team uncovered a coordination post called "Satgas S" located near Melawai Raya in South Jakarta, housed in a property owned by former intelligence chief Solomon Ponto and officially registered to the Strategic Intelligence Agency (Bais). The operation involved approximately 13 people who conducted extensive surveillance of Yunus from multiple locations, including his office at YLBHI and Selyos, tracking his movements before the attack occurred. This level of coordination and the use of state facilities suggests the attack was not the work of rogue individuals but part of a larger intelligence operation.

The response from military authorities raises serious questions about accountability and transparency. While TNI's military police quickly identified four suspects from Bais, civil society groups and investigators express concern that this represents an attempt to limit responsibility to low-level operatives while protecting the command structure. The timing of the military's announcement, made before police had completed their investigation, and the refusal to release photos or full names of the suspects, adds to suspicions of a cover-up. President Prabowo's instruction to investigate intellectual actors behind the attack has yet to be meaningfully implemented, despite his public statements demanding a thorough investigation.

The broader context reveals a pattern of intimidation against civil society that has intensified since the August 2023 unrest investigations. Kontras and other human rights organizations have faced escalating threats following their documentation of military involvement in civilian affairs. This represents a continuation of historical patterns where military intelligence has targeted activists, journalists, and civil society leaders who challenge military expansion or document human rights violations. The attack on Yunus appears designed to silence criticism of military reform and intimidate other activists from continuing their advocacy work.

## Implications

This case represents a critical test for Indonesia's democratic institutions and civilian oversight of the military. The involvement of state facilities and personnel in targeting a prominent human rights activist directly challenges Indonesia's commitment to protecting civil liberties and maintaining civilian supremacy over military forces. If the investigation fails to expose the full command chain and only prosecutes low-level operatives, it will signal that military intelligence operates with impunity and can intimidate civil society without meaningful consequences, potentially encouraging further attacks on activists and journalists.
