## Overview
This analysis from Harian Kompas examines President Prabowo Subianto's political position one year into his presidency, focusing on coalition party support for his potential 2029 re-election bid and the fragility of his current high public satisfaction ratings. Political analyst Yunarto Wijaya discusses how parties like PKB and Golkar have already declared support for Prabowo's second term, while notably excluding Vice President Gibran Rakabuming from their endorsements. The discussion reveals significant vulnerabilities in Prabowo's seemingly strong political position.

## Key Highlights
Yunarto argues that the early party support for Prabowo reflects a typical "honeymoon period" phenomenon where coalition parties automatically back the incumbent president, similar to what occurred with previous presidents like SBY in 2005. However, he emphasizes that this support remains fragile and untested. The analyst points to concerning economic indicators including a weakening rupiah reaching 17,000 per dollar, rising inflation at 2.22% in January, and declining ratings from financial markets as potential threats to Prabowo's popularity. He particularly highlights the ambitious and expensive flagship programs like the Free Nutritious Meals program (costing 335 trillion rupiah) and the Red and White Cooperatives initiative as fiscally risky ventures that could backfire if they fail to deliver results.

The discussion reveals internal dynamics suggesting Prabowo operates with less tolerance for criticism compared to previous presidents, creating what Yunarto describes as a "wall-like" atmosphere where ministers and allies appear reluctant to offer frank feedback. This contrasts with previous administrations where cabinet members felt more comfortable providing critical input. The analyst suggests this rigid approach, combined with Prabowo's unusually ambitious timeline for implementing major programs, creates additional political risks. Meanwhile, the notable exclusion of Vice President Gibran from party endorsements signals that coalition parties view him as politically weak without his father Jokowi's direct backing, preferring to position their own leaders for the vice presidency.

## Implications
These findings suggest Prabowo's path to a second term may be more challenging than current high approval ratings indicate, with economic pressures and program implementation risks creating significant vulnerabilities. The analysis reveals how Indonesia's political landscape remains highly dynamic, with party loyalties and public satisfaction subject to rapid shifts based on economic performance and policy outcomes.
