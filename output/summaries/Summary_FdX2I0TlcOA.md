## Overview
President Prabowo Subianto held a media meeting addressing recent demonstrations and public demands, particularly regarding the events of 28-29 (likely referring to recent protest incidents). The President expressed support for forming an independent investigation team while defending his government's economic achievements and anti-corruption stance. He emphasized that peaceful demonstrations are constitutionally protected but criticized those who incite violence, calling them unpatriotic and accusing them of attempting to pit citizens against leadership for unclear motives.

## Key Highlights

Prabowo demonstrated a measured response to public demands stemming from recent demonstrations, acknowledging that many of the protesters' demands were reasonable and normative. He expressed openness to establishing an independent investigation team for the incidents of 28-29, stating this was a sensible proposal that could be discussed and implemented in an appropriate form. The President firmly defended the right to peaceful demonstration as guaranteed by law, emphasizing that gathering and expressing opinions is legitimate but must remain peaceful and follow established regulations. He noted that demonstrations outside of permitted hours (after 6:00 PM) technically violate existing rules and regulations.

The President addressed criticism regarding the role of security forces, defending both police and military involvement in civilian security. He argued that proportional action by security personnel is essential, with accountability measures already in place for those who exceed their authority. Regarding calls to withdraw the military from civilian security duties, Prabowo questioned the logic of such demands, asserting that the military's role is to protect citizens from various threats including terrorism, arson, and civil unrest. He emphasized that creating jobs and providing social safety nets for the most vulnerable populations remains a government priority, claiming significant progress in employment creation with substantial future potential.

On the broader political landscape, Prabowo expressed confidence in Indonesia's economic and political fundamentals, citing stable economic indicators despite recent protest-related disruptions. He highlighted concrete achievements of his administration, particularly the free nutritious meals program which has reached 25 million beneficiaries and is projected to reach 82.9 million by December or January. The President criticized what he termed "artificial crises" and accused certain parties of attempting to undermine public confidence in the government, while reaffirming his commitment to anti-corruption efforts and protecting national assets from illegal activities.

## Implications
These statements reflect Prabowo's attempt to balance acknowledgment of legitimate public concerns with firm defense of government security policies and achievements. His response suggests the administration is trying to maintain stability while addressing public dissent, though his characterization of critics as unpatriotic may indicate growing political tensions in Indonesian society.
