## Overview
President Prabowo Subianto held a media meeting with journalist Najwa Shihab to address public demonstrations and riots that occurred on January 28-29, 2025. The President responded to various public demands including calls for no criminalization of demonstrators, formation of an independent investigation team, and withdrawal of military forces from civilian security operations. Prabowo defended his administration's achievements while acknowledging ongoing economic challenges and promised continued anti-corruption efforts.

## Key Highlights

President Prabowo demonstrated a measured response to recent public unrest, acknowledging that many demonstrator demands were reasonable while maintaining firm boundaries on law and order. He expressed support for forming an independent investigation team into the January 28-29 incidents and emphasized that peaceful demonstration is a constitutional right that must be protected. However, he clarified that demonstrations outside the legally permitted hours (after 6 PM) violate regulations, and stressed that all protests must remain peaceful and follow proper procedures. The President also defended the role of military forces in civilian security, arguing that the TNI's duty is to protect citizens from various threats including terrorism and riots.

The President highlighted significant progress in his administration's flagship programs, particularly the free nutritious meals initiative which has reached 25 million beneficiaries and is expected to reach 82.9 million by December 2025. He emphasized that economic indicators remain stable despite recent political turbulence, with rural economies showing rapid development and farmer exchange rates improving significantly. Prabowo attributed these improvements to job creation efforts by his government, while acknowledging that employment challenges persist in certain regions and demographics. He criticized what he termed "artificial crises" created by parties seeking to undermine public confidence in the government.

Throughout his remarks, President Prabowo maintained a combative stance against corruption while calling for national unity. He stressed his commitment to anti-corruption efforts and protecting state assets from illegal activities, including unauthorized plantations and mining operations. The President urged all parties to prioritize national interests over group interests and criticized those who seek to incite public anger rather than propose constructive solutions. He concluded by emphasizing that violence is counterproductive and calling for calm, intelligent cooperation in addressing the nation's challenges.

## Implications
This media appearance represents Prabowo's attempt to balance showing responsiveness to public concerns while maintaining authority during a period of social unrest. His mixed approach of accepting some demands while rejecting others, particularly regarding military involvement in civilian affairs, suggests ongoing tensions between democratic accountability and security concerns that will likely continue to shape Indonesian political discourse.
