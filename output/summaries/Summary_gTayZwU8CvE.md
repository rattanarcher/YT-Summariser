The conversation reveals that victims immediately contacted police after recognizing the incident as an intelligence operation, based on patterns similar to past cases including the Amboan incident, Poso conflicts, Operation Green Dragon, Munir's assassination, and Novel Baswedan's acid attack. # Key Highlights

The speakers establish that they immediately recognized the incident as an intelligence operation based on established patterns from Indonesia's history of state surveillance and intimidation. They contacted police the same night, requesting that the crime scene and evidence be secured. The identification was made possible through recognizing similarities to previous high-profile cases, including the Amboan incident, conflicts in Poso, Operation Green Dragon (Operasi Naga Hijau), the assassination of human rights activist Munir, and the acid attack on corruption investigator Novel Baswedan. According to the speakers, these incidents share common characteristics and operational methods that suggest state involvement.

The discussion reveals concerns that the current situation extends beyond just one individual named Andri, who was apparently at the Fairmont hotel during the incident. The speakers suggest there may be other targets involved and express worry that this represents part of a systematic campaign of intimidation. They emphasize that without comprehensive investigation and complete resolution, such incidents will continue to occur as part of an ongoing pattern of terror against political activists and opposition figures.

# Implications

This discussion highlights ongoing concerns about state surveillance and intimidation tactics against political opposition and civil society activists in Indonesia. The systematic nature described suggests potential implications for democratic space and civil liberties, particularly if intelligence operations are being used to suppress political dissent.
