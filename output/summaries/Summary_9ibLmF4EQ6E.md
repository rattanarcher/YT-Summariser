## Overview
President Prabowo participated in the second installment of "Presiden Prabowo Menjawab" hosted by Najwa Shihab, engaging in detailed discussions with six experts on various national issues. The conversation covered his long-held views on food and energy security in the context of current global conflicts, his administration's efforts to combat false reporting and bureaucratic inefficiency, responses to recent attacks on activists, and Indonesia's controversial participation in the Board of Peace (BOP) initiative for Gaza. The discussion revealed Prabowo's strategic thinking on industrialization as a solution to unemployment among educated youth and his commitment to maintaining democratic freedoms while addressing security concerns.

## Key Highlights

Prabowo defended his decades-long emphasis on food and energy security, explaining that his predictions about global conflicts stemmed from historical analysis and "common sense" rather than specific war predictions. He cited the ancient Greek historian Thucydides' principle that "the strong will do what they can, the weak suffer what they must," arguing that Indonesia must prepare for potential conflicts while working toward peace. The President detailed his administration's response to current global tensions, including plans to rapidly expand solar energy capacity to 100 gigawatts within two years and transition all vehicles to electric power to reduce dependency on imported fuel.

The President addressed criticism about false reporting within government institutions, revealing specific examples of bureaucratic inefficiency and corruption. He cited the pupuk (fertilizer) distribution system as emblematic of broader problems, where 145 different regulations required multiple ministerial signatures before subsidized fertilizer could reach farmers. His administration has eliminated these regulations and closed over 1,030 problematic Makan Bergizi Gratis (MBG) kitchens while implementing certification standards for the remaining facilities. Prabowo emphasized his commitment to receiving accurate information, even when unfavorable, and described his practice of monitoring critical coverage on social media, including content from his harshest critics.

Regarding the recent acid attack on activist Andri Yunus, Prabowo made strong commitments to pursue the case to its conclusion, including any state actors who might be involved. He characterized the attack as "terrorism" and "barbaric," promising no impunity for perpetrators regardless of their institutional affiliation. On Indonesia's participation in the Board of Peace initiative, Prabowo provided detailed background on how the country became involved through discussions with President Trump and eight Muslim nations, but confirmed that Indonesia has suspended its participation following the resumption of military actions, stating the initiative is currently "on hold" pending clearer developments.

## Implications

These statements signal Prabowo's intention to accelerate Indonesia's energy independence amid global instability while maintaining a reformist approach to government inefficiency. His strong stance on protecting democratic critics, combined with detailed explanations of Indonesia's complex diplomatic positioning on Gaza, suggests an administration balancing domestic governance reforms with careful international engagement during a period of heightened global tensions.
