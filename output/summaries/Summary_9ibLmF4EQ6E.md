## Overview
This video features President Prabowo participating in a roundtable discussion called "Presiden Prabowo Menjawab" hosted by Najwa Shihab. Six experts questioned the President on various critical topics including food and energy security, government efficiency, the Makan Bergizi Gratis (MBG) program, recent attacks on activists, Indonesia's role in the Board of Peace for Palestine, and economic challenges. The discussion reveals Prabowo's strategic thinking on national transformation, his commitment to industrialization and energy independence, and his responses to criticism about governance and human rights concerns.

## Key Highlights

President Prabowo defended his long-standing advocacy for food and energy security, explaining that his predictions about global conflict were based on historical patterns and common sense rather than specific foresight. He emphasized that wars throughout history have fundamentally been about competition for resources - food, water, and fertile land. Drawing on examples from current conflicts in Ukraine and the Middle East, he demonstrated how global interconnectedness means that regional wars now affect food and energy prices worldwide. His philosophy centers on the principle that true independence requires self-sufficiency in basic necessities, a view he says was shared by Indonesia's founding father Sukarno.

Addressing concerns about false reporting and bureaucratic inefficiency, Prabowo acknowledged that Indonesia suffers from a culture of "ABS" (Asal Bapak Senang - "as long as the boss is happy") where subordinates provide misleadingly positive reports. He cited concrete examples, including discovering 145 overlapping regulations governing fertilizer distribution that prevented subsidized fertilizers from reaching farmers. The President emphasized his commitment to accepting harsh truths and criticism, even monitoring social media content that attacks him personally, as he believes this helps him stay alert to potential problems. He has already implemented massive deregulation efforts and efficiency measures that saved 308 trillion rupiah in the first year.

The President outlined his ambitious industrialization strategy, describing it as a national transformation rather than mere development. He criticized Indonesia's historical role as a raw material exporter, arguing that the country should process its abundant natural resources domestically to create high-quality jobs. His vision includes establishing hundreds of factories across various "industrial trees" for commodities like coconut, coffee, and cocoa. He announced plans to build ten new universities with medical schools to address Indonesia's shortage of 140,000 doctors, and revealed his commitment to transitioning the entire transportation sector to electric vehicles powered by solar energy within two years, aiming for 100 gigawatts of solar capacity.

## Implications

This discussion reveals Prabowo's comprehensive approach to addressing Indonesia's structural economic challenges through rapid industrialization and energy transition. His emphasis on self-reliance in food and energy takes on greater urgency given current global conflicts and their economic impacts. However, his handling of questions about activist intimidation and democratic space, while providing strong verbal commitments, will require concrete follow-through to address growing concerns about shrinking civil liberties under his administration.
