## Overview
This episode of Mata Najwa features heart-wrenching interviews with flood victims in Aceh, particularly focusing on a mother who lost her 9-year-old son Zaid Ali during devastating floods on November 26th. Host Najwa Shihab speaks directly with survivors in the affected area, documenting the human cost of the disaster that claimed 21 lives from two villages with a combined population of 756 people. The episode provides raw, emotional testimony about the tragedy while highlighting the urgent humanitarian needs of the survivors.

## Key Highlights

The most devastating story comes from a mother who watched helplessly as her 9-year-old son Zaid Ali was swept away by floodwaters right before her eyes. She describes the horrific moment when the floods hit at 7 AM on November 26th, forcing her to make an impossible choice between saving her young son or her daughter. Clinging to a cherry tree for 14 hours until rescue arrived at 9 PM, she had to hold tightly to her daughter while her son called out "Mother, please take my hand" before being swept away. The boy's body was found five days later in a rice field, so badly decomposed that she initially couldn't recognize him. Despite her unimaginable loss, she carries his belongings everywhere and speaks with remarkable faith, saying "he was handsome, but Allah has called him" and expressing hope that her missing husband might still return.

The broader impact of the disaster becomes clear through conversations with local officials who report that 21 people died in the floods, with 20 bodies recovered and one person still missing. Nine people were successfully rescued by search and rescue teams and the military from rice fields and trees where they had taken refuge. The community is struggling with basic sanitation needs, with only one emergency toilet facility built for the hundreds of survivors. Village leaders emphasize that proper toilet facilities are desperately needed to prevent disease outbreaks, as human waste is scattered throughout the affected areas.

## Implications

This episode highlights the ongoing vulnerability of rural Indonesian communities to natural disasters and the inadequate disaster preparedness infrastructure. The urgent need for basic sanitation facilities in the aftermath demonstrates gaps in emergency response capabilities that could lead to secondary health crises if not addressed quickly.
