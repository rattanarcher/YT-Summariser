## Overview

This video features popular Indonesian YouTube host Najwa Shihab interviewing the girl group NONA, managed by 88rising, during their first major Indonesian television interview. The conversation covers their journey from training to international success, their creative process, and deeply personal moments including emotional discussions about family loss and homesickness. The interview showcases NONA's role as Indonesian cultural ambassadors on the global stage while revealing their individual personalities and close bond as a group.

## Key Highlights

The interview reveals NONA's deliberate positioning as a distinctly Indonesian girl group rather than following the K-pop model. The members explain how from the beginning, both they and their management at 88rising envisioned creating a global girl group that would bring new sounds and aesthetics while incorporating as many Indonesian elements as possible into their work. They emphasize their mission to represent Indonesian girls who "can make it out there" internationally, following in the footsteps of artists like Niki and Brian who paved the way for Indonesian artists globally.

The conversation becomes particularly emotional when discussing personal sacrifices and family connections. Member Ade shares a touching story about her late father, who passed away four years ago, and how he once told her while watching Niki perform that she too could make it internationally with a foreign company. She reveals that her father was musically inclined and always supported her dreams, making her current success with 88rising feel like a fulfillment of his prophecy. The discussion also touches on a recently deceased family member who had expressed wanting to see Ade perform before passing away, adding layers of poignancy to their success story.

The group discusses their creative involvement in their music, describing an open collaborative process with their management where they help select songs from dozens of options and explore different genres together. Each member has found their strength within the group - Esther dominates in music and lyrics, one member handles choreography, Christy focuses on creative concepts, and another works on melodies. They emphasize how their different backgrounds and strengths complement each other without creating conflict, and hint at upcoming projects that will show "a completely different side" of NONA.

## Implications

This interview positions NONA as significant cultural ambassadors for Indonesia on the global stage, representing a new generation of Indonesian artists who can compete internationally while maintaining their cultural identity. Their success with 88rising and emphasis on Indonesian representation suggests a growing trend of Southeast Asian artists breaking into Western markets on their own terms rather than adopting foreign cultural models like K-pop.
