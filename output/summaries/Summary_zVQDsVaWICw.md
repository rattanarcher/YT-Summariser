## Overview
Indonesian girl group Nona appeared on Najwa Shihab's "Musyawarah" talk show for their first major Indonesian television interview since their global debut with 88rising. The four-member group, currently based in Los Angeles but originally from Jakarta and Lombok, discussed their journey from local trainees to international artists representing Indonesia on the global stage. The emotional interview covered their creative process, upcoming projects, and personal sacrifices made for their music careers.

## Key Highlights
The group emphasized their commitment to incorporating Indonesian cultural elements into their music and maintaining their identity as representatives of Indonesian girls who "can make it out there." They described their collaborative creative process with 88rising, explaining how they filter through dozens of songs to select their releases and participate actively in discussions about genre direction, including R&B and hip-hop influences. The members revealed they each contribute different strengths - Esther handles music and lyrics, one member focuses on choreography, Christy leads creative concepts, and another creates melodies. They stressed that their open communication prevents conflicts during the creative process.

The interview took an emotional turn when the members discussed personal losses that motivated their careers. One member tearfully shared how her late cousin expressed his dying wish to see her perform, while another spoke about how her deceased father, also a musician, had predicted her success after watching Niki's international breakthrough. These personal stories highlighted the sacrifices and emotional weight behind their pursuit of global success. The group acknowledged feeling immense pressure during their two-year training period but expressed current happiness with their achievements while remaining grounded through remembering their humble beginnings.

## Implications
Nona's success represents a significant moment for Indonesian soft power projection, as they serve as cultural ambassadors introducing Indonesian identity to global audiences through accessible digital platforms. Their emphasis on staying true to Indonesian roots while achieving international success could inspire other local artists to pursue global opportunities without abandoning their cultural identity.
