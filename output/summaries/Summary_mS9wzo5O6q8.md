## Overview

In this video from Akbar Faizal Uncensored, the host recounts a personal anecdote about B.J. Habibie's commitment to press freedom during his presidency. The story illustrates how Habibie, after taking power following the restrictive New Order era under Suharto, genuinely implemented press freedom by allowing media to publish unflattering content about him without interference. The host describes visiting Habibie's home with a magazine proof that featured an intentionally unflattering photograph of the president, expecting pushback, but instead finding Habibie praised the photographer's skill in capturing such a candid shot.

## Key Highlights

The narrative centers on a specific incident when the host visited President Habibie's residence with a pre-publication proof of "Wajah Forum" magazine. The publication had been launched with former Tempo magazine staff who were unemployed after their publication was banned during the Suharto era. The magazine's photographer had deliberately selected the most unflattering photograph of Habibie available - one showing him with an awkward facial expression and his mouth agape. The host was uncertain whether to proceed with the publication, concerned about potential governmental backlash against the new magazine.

However, Habibie's reaction completely defied expectations. Rather than demanding the photograph be changed or expressing displeasure, the president actually complimented the photographer's technical skill, saying the photographer was "extraordinary" for being able to capture such a candid image of him. This response demonstrated Habibie's genuine commitment to press freedom, showing that he was willing to accept unflattering coverage as part of a free media landscape. His reaction stood in stark contrast to the media restrictions and government sensitivity that had characterized the previous New Order regime under Suharto.

## Implications

This anecdote reinforces Habibie's historical reputation as a democratizing force in Indonesian politics, particularly regarding media freedom after decades of authoritarian rule. The story illustrates the practical implementation of press freedom beyond mere policy declarations, showing how Habibie's personal tolerance for criticism helped establish a new media environment in post-Suharto Indonesia.
