## Overview

This episode of Mata Najwa featured a special women's edition conversation between host Najwa Shihab and three prominent Indonesian entertainers: singers Anggun, Rossa, and actress-singer Maudy Ayunda. The wide-ranging discussion explored themes of personal transformation, career evolution, the pressures of being public figures, marriage perspectives, and social media's impact on modern life. The conversation provided intimate insights into how these successful women navigate expectations, maintain authenticity, and continue growing both personally and professionally in Indonesia's spotlight.

## Key Highlights

The discussion revealed significant insights into how these public figures have evolved over time. Anggun shared her recent decision to split her time between Paris and Indonesia after 30 years abroad, describing how returning has made her more emotionally expressive and connected to her Indonesian roots. She emphasized the importance of embracing differences rather than competing with other artists, advocating for a mindset that celebrates uniqueness rather than conformity. Rossa demonstrated her philosophy of non-competitiveness, consistently supporting younger artists and sharing opportunities rather than viewing them as threats. Her approach of collaborative leadership and mentorship has made her a respected figure who actively elevates others in the industry.

Maudy Ayunda discussed the challenges of maintaining authenticity while meeting public expectations as a public figure. She described feeling pressured to always be "politically correct" and having to navigate increasingly specific public expectations about how she should respond to various situations. The conversation touched on the burden of being seen as a role model and the difficulty of balancing genuine self-expression with public responsibility. All three women addressed the "marriage is scary" social media narrative, offering nuanced perspectives on relationships, independence, and the importance of finding the right partner rather than rushing into marriage due to societal pressure.

## Implications

This conversation highlights evolving attitudes among successful Indonesian women toward career, relationships, and public life, reflecting broader generational shifts in expectations and values. The discussion of cancel culture, social media pressures, and the challenge of maintaining authenticity suggests these issues are increasingly relevant for public figures in Indonesia's digital age.
