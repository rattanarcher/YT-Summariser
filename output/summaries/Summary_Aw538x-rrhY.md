## Overview

This video discusses the acid attack on human rights activist Andri Yunus from Kontras, who was doused with corrosive liquid after participating in a podcast about military reform and TNI law revision. Host Akbar Faizal interviews Usman Hamid (Amnesty International Indonesia) and Fadil Alfathan (Legal Aid Institute) about the systematic intimidation of activists and shrinking democratic space in Indonesia.

## Key Participants

- **Akbar Faizal**: Host, former legislator and civil society figure
- **Usman Hamid**: Executive Director of Amnesty International Indonesia
- **Fadil Alfathan**: Director of Legal Aid Institute (LBHI), part of Andri Yunus's legal team

## Analysis & Implications

This case represents a concerning escalation in attacks against Indonesian civil society, particularly those critiquing military expansion into civilian affairs. The systematic nature of intimidation - from legal persecution to physical violence - suggests coordinated efforts to silence democratic opposition. The international attention from UN human rights officials indicates growing concern about Indonesia's democratic backsliding, potentially affecting its international standing and leadership roles in regional human rights mechanisms.
