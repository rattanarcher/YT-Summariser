# Presiden Prabowo Menjawab (PART 2): Efisiensi, Pertaruhan MBG & Reformasi Kepolisian | Mata Najwa
**Channel:** Najwa Shihab
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=65uf6jHMCEI

## Overview
This is the second part of a televised discussion where President Prabowo Subianto engaged in an extensive Q&A session with journalists and analysts on Najwa Shihab's program. The discussion covered Indonesia's foreign policy challenges, economic policies including the ART agreement with the US, and domestic programs like the Free Nutritious Meals (MBG) initiative.

## Key Participants
- **Host:** Najwa Shihab
- **Main Guest:** President Prabowo Subianto
- **Panel:** Bang Celie (foreign policy analyst), Nana (journalist), Faisal (economist from CORE), Dede (economic analyst), and Mardigu (analyst)

## Key Points & Timestamps

- [00:35] **Indonesia's Constitutional Foreign Policy**: Discussion of the fourth principle in Indonesia's preamble requiring active participation in world peace, making Indonesia unique among nations with international principles embedded in its constitution

- [03:13] **Indonesia's Geopolitical Position**: Prabowo describes current era as both "second cold war" and "hot peace," emphasizing Indonesia's unique position as the world's fourth-largest population with vast natural resources requiring engagement with all major powers

- [08:29] **Geopolitics as Geoeconomics**: President explains how geopolitical relationships directly impact economic opportunities, citing recent trade agreements with EU and Canada that opened markets for Indonesian products

- [14:20] **Non-Aligned Foreign Policy**: Prabowo reaffirms Indonesia's commitment to non-alignment, stating Indonesia will not choose sides between US and China, maintaining equal relations with all powers

- [19:31] **Red Lines in Foreign Policy**: President clarifies Indonesia will never allow foreign military bases on its territory, a consistent policy since independence, while remaining open to peaceful visits from all nations

- [21:15] **BOP Financial Clarification**: Prabowo definitively states Indonesia made no financial commitment to the Board of Peace initiative, only offering peacekeeping troops, not the rumored $1 billion contribution

- [27:29] **Economic Opportunities from Conflict**: Discussion of how Middle East tensions could benefit Indonesia's sovereign wealth fund (Nusantara Fund) as regional financial centers face instability

- [34:43] **ART Agreement Defense**: Detailed explanation of the Agreement on Reciprocal Tariff with the US, emphasizing 1,819 commodities receiving 0% tariff while maintaining Indonesia's regulatory sovereignty

- [49:38] **Education Infrastructure**: Major announcement of expanding school rehabilitation from 17,000 to 70,000 schools annually, with plans for 300,000 schools over five years, plus digital education through Interactive Flat Panels in all Indonesian schools

- [53:50] **MBG Employment Impact**: Detailed breakdown of job creation from Free Nutritious Meals program - 1.5 million direct jobs in kitchens, another 1.5 million in supply chains, totaling 3 million jobs

- [1:10:21] **MBG Defense**: Strong defense of continuing the nutritious meals program despite criticism, citing Rockefeller Institute finding that each dollar spent returns $7-35 in economic benefit

- [1:22:39] **Police Reform Commitment**: President commits to implementing police reform recommendations from Professor Jimli's committee, emphasizing the need for clean, professional law enforcement institutions

## Analysis & Implications

This extensive interview reveals Prabowo's firm commitment to maintaining Indonesia's non-aligned foreign policy while aggressively pursuing economic transformation through massive infrastructure and social programs. His defense of the controversial MBG program despite fiscal pressures demonstrates his populist approach and willingness to stake his presidency on direct welfare delivery. The clarifications on the ART agreement and BOP initiative show efforts to counter domestic criticism while maintaining strategic partnerships with major powers.
